## [Euclidean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_distance)

Straight-line distance (good for physical space).

$$
D_{\text{Euclidean}}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) = \sqrt{ \sum_{i=1}^{n} (p_i - q_i)^2 }
$$

## [Manhattan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxicab_geometry)

Grid-based movement (like walking city blocks).

$$
D_{\text{Manhattan}}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} |p_i - q_i|
$$

## [Chebyshev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chebyshev_distance)

maximum difference along any coordinate dimension between two vectors.

$$
D_{\text{Chebyshev}}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) = \max_{i=1}^{n} |p_i - q_i|
$$

## [Minkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minkowski_distance)

Generalization of all the above.

- $p = 1$ is Manhattan
- $p = 2$ is Euclidean
- $p \approx \inf$ is Chebyshev

$$
D_{\text{Minkowski}}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) = \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} |p_i - q_i|^p \right)^{\frac{1}{p}}
$$
